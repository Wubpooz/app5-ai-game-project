Methodology & Mathematical Explanations

1. Sequential Probability Ratio Test (SPRT)

The SPRT evaluates whether we can accept the alternative hypothesis H1: Elo difference ≥ 35 versus the null hypothesis H0: Elo difference ≤ 0.

We use a pentanomial (5-outcome pair) model with error bounds α = β = 0.05. A Wald normal approximation is used to estimate the log-likelihood ratio (LLR), preventing inconclusive results caused by observed-mean bias.

2. Chi-Squared (χ2) Homogeneity

The homogeneity test measures whether win, loss, and draw rates are independent of the opening played. Because search engines are entirely deterministic, playing the same opening multiple times always results in the identical outcome (zero variance per opening).

When outcomes vary across openings, the trials perfectly cluster, driving χ2 to its mathematical maximum (500) and yielding a p-value of 0.0000. When outcomes are identical, observed equals expected perfectly, yielding p-value = 1.0000.

3. SPSA Parameter Optimization

Simultaneous Perturbation Stochastic Approximation (SPSA) optimizes the 9 heuristic weights simultaneously. SPSA perturbs all parameters by a random vector Δ ∈ {-1, +1}9 and runs parallel game matches to estimate the performance gradient.

Tuning proved that standing on 1-band squares restricts the engine's own mobility too much (reversing weightBandControl to -34), while restricting the opponent on 1-band squares remains critical (weightOppBandControl = 85).
